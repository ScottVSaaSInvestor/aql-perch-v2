What is PERCH?

From the eagle's perch, they can assess and analyze.

That's what PERCH does. It helps analyze the most significant change in software — the emergence of vertical Systems of Action — and its impact on a specific target company.

PERCH answers three questions:

  1. Does this modern SOR have a lane? (Is the category defensible?)
  2. For how long? (What's the competitive window?)
  3. How ready is it to become a System of Action? (How far to the destination?)

Because we don't invest unless we know if — and how — we can help them get there. Before we commit capital.

Why it matters for AQL

The AQL thesis is that vertical software Systems of Record (SORs) undergoing the SOR→SOA transition are where the next decade of MOIC expansion lives. But this transition is not a feature checkbox — it requires the right category (protected moat), the right company (foundation ready to compound), and the right sequencing (Bridge playbook tailored to urgency and feasible speed).

PERCH diagnoses all three, simultaneously, in one evidence-backed instrument. It's how the firm holds itself to a consistent, repeatable bar on AI-era deals.

PERCH is the diagnostic instrument; the AQL AI Risk & Readiness Framework is the underlying methodology.

The Three Statements

"Risk sets urgency. Readiness sets feasible speed. All paths converge on SOA milestones."

Risk = Urgency. How fast the company must ship SOA capability before external forces compress value.

Readiness = Feasible Speed. What the company can actually execute given its foundations.

SOA = Destination. All quadrants converge on the same endpoint. Different postures are different sequencing disciplines — not different destinations.
